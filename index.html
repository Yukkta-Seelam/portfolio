About Me

I am a Master's student in Robotics and Autonomous Systems at Boston University, with a strong academic background in Electrical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur. My research and projects focus on robotics, AI, and machine learning, with experience in obstacle avoidance, path planning, music synthesis, and combat robotics. I have worked on real-world applications such as autonomous rovers, AI combat robots, and audio event detection. I am passionate about leveraging technology to solve complex problems and innovate in the field of robotics.

Projects

Obstacle Avoidance and Path Planning for Rover

Developed an algorithm for obstacle avoidance of the Clearpath Husky rover in an unfamiliar and dynamic environment. Implemented the algorithm in a real-life scenario, tasking the rover with harvesting fruits from apple trees in a garden. Employed selective fine-tuning on YOLOv8 for improved use-case sensitivity.

Music Synthesis using Generative Machine Learning

Created a music generation system leveraging a Variational Auto-Encoder, adapting established western techniques for generating nuanced pitch contours suited for the intricacies of Indian classical music. Pioneered a unique model extracting pitch contours from Indian classical music, preserving note-transitional information.

Development of AI Combat Robots

Engineered autonomous combat robots with advanced AI capabilities, attaining 85% mean Average Precision for real-time enemy detection and 90% accuracy in dynamic target shooting within an eight-meter range. Performed advanced localization and path planning for combat robots in dynamic arenas using visual markers, Eigen transforms, A* planning, and embedded NVIDIA GPUs.

Audio Event Detection

Revised models for accurate multiple audio event detection and classification through cross-correlations. Utilized Mel-filter banks, K-class Discriminant to extract MFCC features, achieving precise audio classification across 10 distinct classes. Deployed LSTM for tagging audio samples into similar 10 classes at each time step.

Rover Development

Constructed a customizable research robot, including power management, battery management, and motor control modules. Integrated sub-modules using open-source software, programmed micro-controller, and enabled remote control operation. Simulated a model for three-degrees-of-freedom gripper arm with a 3Kg payload.
